The Cultivater Editorial Planning

The Cultivater Launch Sprint Brief

Prepared for review on May 17, 2026

Working Objective

Turn The Cultivater from a concept and contact list into a focused first editorial sprint with a clear subject mix, a first-wave target list, and a clean division of work between Asher and Venetia.

Editorial Position

The Cultivater should present sustainability as a more beautiful, tactile, flavorful, and enduring way of living rather than as a guilt-driven obligation. The core lens is provenance, stewardship, longevity, and design quality. The tone should stay elegant, practical, and aspirational.

North star: document what is being found, not only what is being lost. The magazine should highlight the makers, growers, designers, hosts, and systems thinkers building a better version of daily life.

Launch Sprint Structure

The first sprint should be organized around six pillars:

  1. Organic Farming
  2. Sustainable Fashion
  3. Green Design
  4. Food & Drink
  5. Arts & Travel
  6. Legislation & Systems

Recommended First Wave

Pillar Recommended First Target Editorial Angle
Organic Farming Propagate Soil, stewardship, cultivation methods, and the long-view relationship between grower and landscape.
Sustainable Fashion Kotn or Pangaia Material choices, humane production, and how sustainability can still feel luxurious and desirable.
Green Design Terrapin Bright Green Design as lived experience: how healthier spaces, better materials, and beauty reinforce one another.
Food & Drink SingleThread Hospitality, farming, seasonality, and the dignity of craft at the table.
Arts & Travel Zannier Hotels Slow travel, place-based design, and cultural experience shaped by restraint and quality.
Legislation & Systems Open slot A Venetia-led explainer or interview with a standards, circularity, policy, NGO, or legal expert.

Role Split

Asher

Asher should own the heritage, provenance, organic farming, hospitality, food and drink, stewardship, and slow-travel spine of the sprint. His pieces should carry warmth, lineage, texture, and a sense of calm authority.

Venetia

Venetia should own sustainable fashion, green design, circular systems, legislation, and the future-facing sustainability frame. Her pieces should sharpen the material, systems, and design-forward lens without flattening the magazine into policy jargon.

Shared Rule

Both editors should avoid guilt-based framing. The Cultivater voice should invite readers into a richer and better-made world, not scold them into compliance.

Immediate Sprint Outputs

  • Approve or revise the six first-wave targets.
  • Choose the Legislation & Systems target or approve a Venetia-led explainer for that slot.
  • Prepare company-specific outreach drafts for permission, interview, or feature requests.
  • Have Asher and Venetia draft one pilot piece each from the approved first wave.
  • Review the voice and structure before expanding the sprint into a broader editorial calendar.

Open Decision

The only unresolved gap in the first wave is the Legislation & Systems slot. If there is a preferred standards body, NGO, policy expert, legal specialist, or circularity leader already in the broader contact universe, that should fill the sixth position. Otherwise, Venetia should draft the first systems explainer without waiting for a company interview.

Next Action

Use this brief as the clean review document, then turn the approved first-wave list into send-ready outreach drafts and two pilot article assignments.
