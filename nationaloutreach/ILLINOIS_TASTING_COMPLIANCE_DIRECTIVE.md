# Illinois Tasting Compliance Directive

Updated: 2026-05-01 CDT
Source: Sebastian Saller guidance and linked Google Drive policy/SOP, verified accessible by National Outreach on 2026-05-01.

## Directive

For Illinois tasting events, first determine the event category and who is supplying the spirits samples.

- If the event organizer or venue supplies the spirits purchased through a distributor, treat the event as not requiring KOVAL to obtain a Craft Distiller Tasting Permit License unless Sebastian or another compliance owner says otherwise.
- Regular retail tastings, including Binny's, Mariano's, Whole Foods, and similar ordinary account tastings, do not need Sebastian notification solely because a tasting is happening.
- If KOVAL supplies or transports spirits samples directly to a non-routine event venue, or if the event category/sample-provider path is unclear, a Craft Distiller Tasting Permit License may be required through the ILCC, and the case should be flagged for Sebastian / CAO review.

Before confirming event logistics, capture these fields in the event record or owner packet:

- event organizer name;
- venue name;
- full venue address, including municipality;
- event date and time;
- whether the event organizer has previously carried KOVAL products;
- whether the venue/event organizer can provide product samples;
- whether KOVAL is expected to supply samples;
- CDTPL required: yes, no, or unknown.

## Timing Rule

Preferred notice for all Illinois tasting events is at least 14 calendar days before the event.

Minimum internal cutoff:

- Chicago events: 2 business days before the event.
- Illinois events outside Chicago: 3 business days before the event.

Events inside the 14-day window may incur the higher ILCC fee and should be flagged early. Events submitted after the minimum cutoff may be denied participation or require executive approval and acceptance of any additional fees.

## Weekly Maintenance

Vanessa / National Outreach should maintain a centralized Illinois tasting event list and run a weekly review of upcoming Illinois events to flag any that may require a CDTPL.

For each upcoming Illinois event, verify:

- location and municipality;
- whether it is a regular retail tasting or a non-routine event;
- who supplies samples;
- permit status;
- deadline status against the 14-day preferred notice and 2/3-business-day cutoff;
- whether Sebastian / CAO has been notified only for permit-required, non-routine, or unclear events.
