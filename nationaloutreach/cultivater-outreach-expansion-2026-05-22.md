## Legislation and Systems Scan

The contact sheet itself does not include a policy-first or standards-first organization, so the open sixth slot should be filled from outside that sheet. The strongest current fits for Venetia's lane are:

1. Textile Exchange
   - Why it fits: direct fashion and materials standards angle, closest match to the contact sheet's sustainable-fashion lane.
   - Current hook: Textile Exchange says its Materials Matter Standard becomes effective on December 31, 2026 and mandatory on December 31, 2027.
   - Best angle: how standards move sustainable fashion from branding language into verifiable material practice.

2. Ellen MacArthur Foundation
   - Why it fits: strongest circular-systems bridge between design, textiles, and policy.
   - Current hook: the foundation's April 20, 2026 paper says the European Commission is set to adopt an EU Circular Economy Act in autumn 2026.
   - Best angle: why circular-economy law is becoming an industrial-design story, not only a waste story.

3. U.S. Green Building Council
   - Why it fits: best built-environment policy counterpart for the green-design pillar.
   - Current hook: USGBC's advocacy materials position it as a public-policy voice for building decarbonization and resource-conscious design.
   - Best angle: how standards, certification, and policy shape healthier spaces without making beauty feel secondary.

## Recommendation

- Use `Textile Exchange` as the preferred legislation/systems target if the goal is to keep the first sprint closest to fashion and materials.
- Use `Ellen MacArthur Foundation` as the preferred backup if Sonat wants a broader circular-economy and policy lens.
- Use `USGBC` only if Sonat wants the sixth slot to lean toward green design and buildings instead of textiles.
