## Quick assessment

The central argument against A/D #3 is strong: current IRS guidance says Section 70605(d) limits ERC credits/refunds for the third and fourth quarters of 2021 when claims were filed after January 31, 2024. Koval's pleaded quarters are the first, second, and fourth quarters of 2020 and the first quarter of 2021, so the OBBB limitation does not appear to fit the pleaded claims.

## Why A/D #2 is not requested "with prejudice"

A/D #2, contributory negligence, is attacked mainly for pleading defects: lack of specific facts, vague "actions," and failure to plead the elements. That kind of defect is often curable by amendment if the defendant can allege concrete facts. Because the defect is not framed as a categorical legal impossibility, asking to strike it without prejudice is more conservative.

By contrast, A/D #1 and A/D #3 are framed as legally defective in a way amendment should not fix: A/D #1 is not mitigation because it concerns pre-injury conduct rather than post-injury minimization of damages; A/D #3 relies on a statute that does not apply to Koval's claimed quarters and, even if it did, would not defeat malpractice liability because filing by the applicable deadline was allegedly L&L's duty.

## Additional items to mark for counsel review

- Paragraph 18 says the quarters "after the date of enactment" are the third and fourth quarters of 2021. That phrasing is vulnerable because the 2021 quarters occurred before the July 4, 2025 enactment date. Consider reframing around the credits/claims affected by § 3134(n) and IRS guidance, not quarters occurring after enactment.
- Paragraph 17 says Section 70605 "has not been codified," but it is being cited through statutory notes to 26 U.S.C. § 3134. Consider: "Section 70605(d) appears in the statutory notes to 26 U.S.C. § 3134" to avoid a technical distraction.
- Paragraph 8 says "plaintiff's A/D seeks"; this appears to mean "defendant's A/D seeks."
- The conclusion says "strike A/D #1 and  #3 stricken with prejudice." Consider: "strike A/D #1 and A/D #3 with prejudice."
- The internal section lettering has two consecutive "B." headings and two consecutive "C." headings. Consider renumbering section headings before filing.
- The quoted defendant language at A/D #3 says "Public Law 19-21." The motion correctly identifies that as a miscite. Do not change the quote unless the underlying amended pleading actually says something different.
