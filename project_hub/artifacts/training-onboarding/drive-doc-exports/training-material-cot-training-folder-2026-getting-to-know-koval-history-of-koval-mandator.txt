﻿A Brief KOVAL History


KOVAL was founded in 2008 by Dr. Sonat Birnecker Hart & Dr. Robert Birnecker. We were actually the first distillery to open in Chicago since the mid-1800s. This was because there were a lot of residual laws left over from the Prohibition Era. In fact, Sonat is responsible for getting laws passed that made it possible for craft distilleries to organize tours, tastings, and retail on site (none of which was legal when they started).


Secondly, the craft distillery boom had not happened yet. When KOVAL began, there were under 50 craft distilleries in the US. Now, there are over 4000.


So, why would Robert and Sonat go into such uncharted territory?


Well, Robert’s Austrian family had been distilling for three generations! At age 7, Robert was helping his grandparents in their award-winning distillery outside of Salzburg. In Austria, the culture of distilling is much more present with over 20,000 distilleries!


Why the name KOVAL?


KOVAL is a name that honors both Robert and Sonat’s heritage. In Yiddish, the word KOVAL is a nickname, and it means blacksmith or someone who FORGES something new (like a blacksmith). Basically, someone who forges ahead, or does something new or out of the ordinary.


Sonat’s Great-grandfather Emmanuel earned KOVAL as his nickname when at the age of 17 he surprised his family and emigrated from Vienna to Chicago in the early 1900s to start a business, thus paving the way for the rest of Sonat’s family.


On Robert’s side, the last name KOVAL is an incredibly common Eastern European surname. Its direct translation is ‘blacksmith’ and is the equivalent in the English speaking world to having the last name Smith. Coincidentally, the surname of Robert's grandfather (at whose side Robert learned the art of distilling) is Schmid - German for "Smith." So, the name KOVAL honors both men – and KOVAL’s founders made an extraordinary decision to leave their careers to make some of the most unique whiskey in America.


Organic!? Kosher!?


We are an ORGANIC and Kosher facility! We never use any artificial flavoring, sweeteners or additives in our products, and locally source our grains from Organic certified farms here in the Midwest.


Organic certification involves rigorous checks that involve making sure that EVERY bottle can be traced back to the field on which the grain is grown and that EVERYTHING from the ground up is certified and up to date with organic practices. No GMO, NO pesticides ever.


Kosher certification has nothing to do with Rabbi's blessings. It is a third-party certification, like organic, that focuses on traceability, transparency, and purity. It also means we would never use bugs for coloring–we do not use any colorings for that matter, but some brands use coloring from bugs– which is not kosher. ;)
