# OPS Market Events Module Outline

Status: planning-only draft
Audience: sales and operations users who need to understand Market Events in OPS
Goal: explain what the system is for, how to navigate it, and how event records connect to day-to-day work

## Scope

This first Trainual module covers the user-facing Market Events workflow at a conceptual and operational level. It should help a sales or operations user answer:

- What is a Market Event?
- Where do I find Market Events in OPS?
- What information should I check before working an event?
- How do linked shifts or staffing details fit into the event record?
- What should I update after the event?
- When should I create or update related accounts, contacts, or activities?
- What should I avoid changing without confirmation?

## Out Of Scope

- Building or changing the OPS events module.
- Recording the video.
- Publishing to Trainual or any external training surface.
- Mutating live event, shift, account, contact, activity, Login, Portal, CRM, or calendar data.
- Explaining internal technical architecture, database tables, credentials, 2FA, or admin-only setup.
- Covering every future Events and Connecteam-replacement workflow in one pass.

## Lesson Sequence

1. **What Market Events Are**
   - Market Events are the working records for tastings, samplings, account events, and related field activity.
   - Emphasize that OPS is the work surface and Salesreport/CRM-style records may be related but should not be casually duplicated.

2. **Finding The Market Events Area**
   - Start from the normal OPS landing page after login.
   - Navigate to the Market Events list or calendar view.
   - Explain any filters that sales users are expected to use, such as date, market, owner, account, or status.

3. **Reading An Event Record**
   - Identify the event name, date/time, location/account, owner, market, staffing, status, and notes.
   - Point out linked shifts or staffing details when present.
   - Explain which fields are normally informational versus fields a user is expected to update.

4. **Preparing For An Event**
   - Confirm account/location, event timing, internal owner, staffing, and any required notes.
   - Check whether contacts, activities, or account context already exist before creating anything new.
   - Confirm unclear event instructions with the owner rather than editing around ambiguity.

5. **After-Event Updates**
   - Record outcome notes in the expected event/activity location.
   - Update status only when the user is sure the event is complete or has changed.
   - Avoid creating duplicate accounts or contacts.

6. **Common Mistakes**
   - Editing the wrong event because filters are still applied.
   - Recording private notes or credentials in event comments.
   - Duplicating accounts/contacts instead of searching first.
   - Treating demo/test data as production training evidence without labeling it.

7. **Where To Get Help**
   - Ask the event owner or OPS coordinator for unclear event ownership, missing account/contact context, or unusual staffing requirements.
   - Escalate access, login, or permission issues rather than sharing credentials.
